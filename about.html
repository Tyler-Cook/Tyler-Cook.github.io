Hello! I am Tyler and am a DMV based Game Developer with expereince in Unreal Engine 5 and Unity 6.

A bit about me... I attended the Fulton School of Engineering at Arizona State University from 2021–2023 studying full stack web development. After starting my Junior year, I got a part-time job at National Technology Integrators (NTI) as an Operations Specialist, which, due to lucky timing, quickly turned into me starting the IT department. I had no experience in IT and am just generally computer-savvy, but due to my development experience, it was a case of computer = computer. It was at this point I was offered a promotion as a System Administrator and made the decision to put school on pause.


After stepping into the System Administrator role, I spent the next two and a half years building out the foundation of our internal IT operations. I introduced new tools, standardized workflows, and created processes that made day-to-day support smoother for our office staff. As the company expanded, I helped lead the onboarding and integration of a newly acquired sister company, making sure their team had a seamless transition into our systems. It’s been incredibly rewarding to watch the department grow from something I helped spark into a fully established part of the organization, and I'm grateful I was able to contribute to NTI's momentum and evolution.


In March of 2025, I made the decision to move on from IT and pursue a goal I always thought was out of reach: Game Development. I returned to school at the University of Maryland Global Campus and will finish my BS in Game Design and Minor in Computer Science in May of 2026. I have engine-shopped quite a bit in the last two years and have experience in Godot, Unity 6, and Unreal Engine 5, with UE5 being my preference and focus point for development.


Games have always been more than entertainment for me. The deeper I’ve gone into development, the more I’ve come to appreciate just how many disciplines come together to bring a single moment of gameplay to life. Art, engineering, design, storytelling, planning, iteration; it’s a collaboration of creativity and problem-solving that's inspiring. Being part of that process motivates me. I want to help create experiences that make people feel something, explore something, or simply escape for a moment. With that drive in mind, I’m seeking game design roles that let me collaborate, learn, and help bring new ideas to life.
